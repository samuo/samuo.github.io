About

Two disciplines, one way of thinking.

I'm a medical student at Columbia University Vagelos College of Physicians & Surgeons (MD candidate, Class of 2029) and a Stanford-trained Human Biologist with a minor in Music. My research lives where physiology meets performance — from salivary mitochondrial DNA and VO₂max in elite athletes to biomarkers for hypertension.

I'm also a jazz pianist. At Stanford I built the university's first performing arts medicine course, and I keep that thread alive at Columbia as Musical Monday Director for the VP&S Musicians' Guild. Improvising at the keyboard and designing a study feel like the same instinct to me: listen closely, test an idea, and stay open to where it leads.
